# Strong Ginger Beer with *S. boulardii*

Yield: ~1 liter
Active time: ~20 minutes
Ferment time: 24–48 hours, plus optional 12–24 hours for bottle carbonation

## Ingredients

- 650 ml non-chlorinated water (filtered, spring, or distilled)
- 150 g fresh ginger, divided (100 g grated for boiling, 50 g juiced for finishing)
- 100 g sugar (coconut or white; bumped from 80 g for better carbonation)
- 1 g *Saccharomyces boulardii* (~4 capsules / 20 billion CFU)
- Juice of half a lemon (~30 ml) — keep it in, not optional
- Optional, for bottle-conditioning: 1 tsp sugar per 500 ml bottle

## Equipment

- Saucepan
- Fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth
- 1 L jar or bottle for primary ferment (a mason jar works well)
- Cloth + rubber band, or airlock lid
- Pressure-rated bottles for carbonation (swing-top / flip-top / thick PET plastic)
- Optional: clean empty plastic soda bottle as a pressure tester

## Instructions

### Primary ferment

1. Bring 650 ml water to a boil in the saucepan.

2. Add 100 g grated ginger, reduce to a simmer, and cook 10 minutes.

3. Remove from heat and stir in 100 g sugar until fully dissolved.

4. Cool completely to room temperature — below ~35°C / 95°F. If it's warm to the touch, it will kill the yeast.

5. Juice the remaining 50 g ginger (grate and squeeze through a fine sieve or cheesecloth) and stir into the cooled liquid.

6. Stir in the lemon juice. Open 4 capsules of *S. boulardii* and stir in the powder.

7. Pour into your fermentation jar. Cover loosely with a cloth and rubber band, or fit an airlock lid.

8. Ferment at room temperature for 24–48 hours. It's ready when pleasantly fizzy and noticeably less sweet than when you started. Warm kitchen = closer to 24 hours; cool kitchen = 48+.

### Optional: bottle-conditioning for real fizz

9. Strain through fine mesh or cheesecloth into a clean container — remove all the ginger solids.

10. Transfer into pressure-rated swing-top bottles. Optionally add 1 tsp sugar per 500 ml bottle to boost carbonation. Leave ~2 cm of headspace.

11. Pressure-check trick: fill a clean plastic soda bottle with some of the liquid alongside your main bottles. When the plastic bottle is rock-hard and can't be squeezed, carbonation is done.

12. Leave at room temperature 12–24 hours, checking the plastic tester bottle daily.

13. Refrigerate as soon as the tester bottle is firm. Cold slows fermentation dramatically and prevents bottle explosions.

## Serving

- Drink ~250 ml per day, ideally 20–30 minutes before a meal.
- Keep bottles cold and drink within 1–2 weeks.
- Open over a sink the first time — fizz can be enthusiastic.

## Notes & safety

- This is a fermented drink and contains some alcohol — expect roughly 1–3% ABV depending on time and temperature.
- Use pressure-rated bottles only. Screw-top glass bottles and thin glass can shatter from CO2 pressure.
- If it tastes vinegary, it went too long. Still drinkable, just past its prime.
- Strain thoroughly before bottling — residual ginger pulp keeps fermenting and makes pouring messy.
- Lemon lowers the pH and helps the yeast dominate over unwanted microbes — worth including.
