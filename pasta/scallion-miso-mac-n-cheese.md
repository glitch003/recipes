# Scallion-Miso Mac and Cheese

🧀 Scallion-Miso Mac and Cheese — Baked Pasta Version

## Ingredients (grams)

- 454 g pasta shells (1 lb)
- 170 g unsalted butter (6 Tbsp, divided)
- 85 g buttery crackers (Ritz-style), finely crushed
- 32 g all-purpose flour (¼ cup)
- 960 g whole milk (4 cups)
- 54 g white miso (3 Tbsp)
- 15 g Dijon mustard (1 Tbsp)
- 225 g sharp Cheddar cheese, grated
- 113 g Gruyère (or Comté), grated
- 28 g Parmesan cheese, grated
- 100 g scallions, finely chopped
- Salt and black pepper, to taste

## Nutrition (estimated)

- **Per full recipe (4 servings)**:  
  **~3,650 kcal** — **Fat 220 g**, **Carbs 295 g**, **Protein 155 g**, **Fiber 12 g**, **Sugar 42 g**, **Sodium ~6,800 mg**

- **Per serving (¼ recipe)**:  
  **~910 kcal** — **Fat 55 g**, **Carbs 74 g**, **Protein 39 g**, **Fiber 3 g**, **Sugar 10.5 g**, **Sodium ~1,700 mg**

- **Assumptions**:
  - Pasta modeled as standard dry wheat pasta
  - Dairy uses **whole milk**, full-fat cheeses, and unsalted butter
  - Crackers modeled as Ritz-style butter crackers
  - Sodium includes miso, cheeses, crackers, and added salt (pepper ignored)
  - Nutrition is an estimate and will vary by brand

## Instructions

1. Heat oven to 375°F (190°C).
2. Cook pasta in well-salted water until just shy of al dente. Drain.
3. Mix 2 Tbsp melted butter with crushed crackers; set aside.
4. In a large Dutch oven, melt remaining butter over medium heat. Whisk in flour and cook 1 minute.
5. Slowly whisk in milk. Add miso, salt, and pepper; simmer until slightly thickened.
6. Remove from heat. Whisk in Dijon, Cheddar, Gruyère, and Parmesan until smooth.
7. Stir pasta into cheese sauce.
8. Mix scallions into cracker topping and scatter evenly over pasta.
9. Bake uncovered for ~20 minutes, until golden and bubbling.
10. Serve warm, garnished with extra scallions and black pepper if desired.
