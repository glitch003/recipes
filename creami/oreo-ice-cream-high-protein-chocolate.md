# Oreo Ice Cream Creami (High Protein — Double Chocolate Version)

Version B of 2. Keeps the Greek yogurt base but uses cocoa and espresso to drown the tang and whey flavor. Leans cheesecake-chocolatey rather than classic cookies-and-cream.

## Ingredients (grams)

- 200 g Greek yogurt (0% fat)
- 45 g milk (whole or 2%)
- 35 g fresh espresso (one double shot), cooled to room temp
- 40 g heavy whipping cream
- 30 g unflavored whey protein powder (or chocolate whey if you have it — skip 3g of the cocoa if so)
- 20 g cream cheese, softened
- 22 g sugar (or sweetener equivalent, to taste)
- 8 g unsweetened cocoa powder (Dutch-process preferred)
- 5 g vanilla extract
- 0.8 g xanthan gum
- 1 g salt
- 40 g Oreo cookies (~4 cookies): 1 cookie finely crushed into the base, 3 cookies roughly crushed and folded in

## Nutrition (estimated)

- **Per pint**: **~790 kcal** — **Fat ~36 g**, **Carbs ~68 g**, **Protein ~53 g**
- **Per 1/2 pint**: **~395 kcal** — **Fat ~18 g**, **Carbs ~34 g**, **Protein ~27 g**
- **Assumptions**: 0% Greek yogurt; whole milk; standard heavy whipping cream; 24g protein per 30g whey scoop; full-fat cream cheese; standard Oreos (~47 kcal each); unsweetened cocoa (~12 kcal/8g); espresso (~negligible kcal).

## Instructions

1. Pull a 35 g double shot of espresso and let it cool to room temp (hot espresso will scramble the cream cheese and taste off).
2. Whisk the cocoa with the sugar first (breaks up lumps so it blends smooth).
3. Add Greek yogurt, milk, cooled espresso, heavy cream, protein powder, cream cheese, vanilla, xanthan gum, salt, and the finely-crushed Oreo. Blend until completely smooth.
4. Taste — it should be noticeably sweet and chocolatey at room temp (freezing dulls both). Add 5g more sugar if flat.
5. Fold in the 3 roughly-crushed Oreos by hand.
6. Pour into the Ninja Creami pint.
7. Freeze for 24 hours.
8. Spin on **Ice Cream**.
9. If powdery, add **15–20 g milk** and **Re-Spin** (yogurt bases need this more often).

## Tasting hypothesis

Real espresso brings coffee aromatics that dry powder can't match — this lands firmly in mocha-oreo territory, and yogurt tang reads as "chocolate cheesecake" under the cocoa and coffee. 22g sugar (up from 20g) compensates for espresso bitterness. Milk was dropped by 35g to make room for the shot so total liquid stays constant.

## Tasting notes

- Not good at all. I used undutched "natural process" cocoa powder and it was very bitter combined with the espresso from my espresso machine. I don't know if it was the cocoa or the espresso but I think it was the cocoa. I will try dutched cocoa next time.
