# Oreo Ice Cream Creami (High Protein — Creamy Version)

Version A of 2. Uses blended whole-milk ricotta as the protein base — richer mouthfeel than Greek yogurt and no tangy/whey-chalk flavor fighting the Oreos. Cannoli-adjacent, in a good way.

## Ingredients (grams)

- 150 g whole-milk ricotta, blended smooth
- 100 g milk (whole)
- 30 g heavy whipping cream
- 40 g unflavored whey protein powder
- 20 g cream cheese, softened
- 20 g sugar (or sweetener equivalent, to taste)
- 5 g vanilla extract
- 0.8 g xanthan gum
- 1 g salt
- 45 g Oreo cookies (~4–5 cookies): 2 cookies finely crushed into the base, the rest roughly crushed and folded in

## Nutrition (estimated)

- **Per pint**: **~890 kcal** — **Fat ~47 g**, **Carbs ~66 g**, **Protein ~51 g**
- **Per 1/2 pint**: **~445 kcal** — **Fat ~23 g**, **Carbs ~33 g**, **Protein ~26 g**
- **Assumptions**: whole-milk ricotta (~147 kcal/100g); whole milk; standard heavy whipping cream; 24g protein per 30g whey scoop; full-fat cream cheese; standard Oreos (~47 kcal each).

## Instructions

1. Blend ricotta, milk, heavy cream, protein powder, cream cheese, sugar, vanilla, xanthan gum, salt, and the 2 finely-crushed Oreos until completely smooth — ricotta should disappear into the mix with no graininess.
2. Fold in the remaining roughly-crushed Oreos by hand (keep them chunky).
3. Pour into the Ninja Creami pint.
4. Freeze for 24 hours.
5. Spin on **Ice Cream**.
6. If powdery, add **10–15 g milk** and **Re-Spin**.

## Tasting hypothesis

Ricotta brings real fat (~28g from the 150g) plus a mild sweet-milky flavor that harmonizes with vanilla and Oreos — way more forgiving than Greek yogurt's tang. The HWC is still useful for mouthfeel but we dropped it to 30g since ricotta is already doing the fat work. Crushing 2 cookies into the base gives Oreo flavor throughout, not just in the chunks.
